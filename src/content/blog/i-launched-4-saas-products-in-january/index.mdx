## \#1. Finally - Option Screener! - [option-screener.com](https://option-screener.com)

I'm going to start of with the biggun' - something that I've had in mind for years now and finally got the time to put together. Building on the success of The Wheel Screener and LEAPS Screener, I'm finally releasing Option Screener - a screener for a variety of strategies across the entire market. This is my sort of 'magnum opus' product, at least in the line of all these screeners I've built. Much more to come here over the coming years and months - if all goes well, I'll have a premium not just options analytics tool, but a full alerts system and trading suite.

## \#2. PriceArb - [pricearb.com](https://pricearb.com)

Coming in at number two, we have PriceArb. This is a very special product in my mind, and I'm either way too early, or so ignorant that I don't understand the sports betting world at all yet. This one was really a fun one and where I had a new breakthrough in my AI workflows. Using claude and my [issue_terminal_command MCP tool], I was able to build out [one of the most comprehensive NFL stat](https://pricearb.com/blog/100-nfl-stats-mad-lads) tables you can find on the web. Unfortunately, the launch flopped completely and I don't see any traction. I'll have to put this one on the backburner for now.

## \#3. Hindsight Data - [hindsightapi.com](https://hindsightapi.com)

Next, we have Hindsight Data - this is a historical api service where you can immediately get the answer to questions like, "what dates were all the FOMC trading dates?", "which days was CPI reported?". Combining this data with something like [AMT JOY](https://amtjoy.com), or a dashboard like the wheel screener, this becomes a powerful analytics and research tool. I've based the pricing off of many similar API datasets found around the web, and I'm hoping to officially launch this some time in February. (This was an experiment of building our first 'API' product - we will move to add an API layer to The Wheel Screener, LEAPS Screener, Option Screener, VannaCharm, and AMTJOY as well in February.)

## \#4. Semanticprint - [semanticprint.com](https://semanticprint.com)

This tool is only in it's early stages - but anyway provides a very cool architecture - full git cloning, file tree display, and shell operations on anything in that given repository. This is much more of an exploratory product than a "go to scale to the moon" one, but that could change as LLMs and agents continue to explode in popularity.

## Building is Outpacing Launching!

The problem is, I'm currently building faster than I can launch or properly market and disseminate new features. Febraury will still be a shipping month for all our APIs, but I think I also need to take a step back a bit and instead focus and push things that are working, and leaving whatever isn't working behind for now.
